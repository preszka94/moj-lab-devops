Trading Desk Operations & Settlement

1. Financial Instruments

  • Equities: Ownership shares in a publicly traded company. They represent a claim on part of the corporation's assets and earnings.
  • Leveraged Products: Instruments like Options, Futures, and Margin trading. They allow traders to gain large market exposure with a relatively small initial capital outlay, amplifying both potential profits and potential losses.

2. The Trade Lifecycle

  1. Execution: The trader agrees to a price and quantity in the market.
  2. Booking: The trade details are captured in the bank's internal risk and position management systems.
  3. Confirmation: Both counterparties verify and agree on the exact details of the executed trade.
  4. Settlement: The actual exchange of the financial instrument for cash (often T+2, meaning two days after the trade date).

3. Troubleshooting: Missing Prices in Horizon GUI

If a product is not displaying a price in the Horizon trading GUI, check the following root causes:

  • Market Data Feed Issue: The connection to the exchange (like Bloomberg or Reuters) may be down or dropping packets.
  • Trading Suspension: The exchange may have halted trading on that specific product due to volatility or pending news.
  • Liquidity Drought: There may be zero active bids or asks in the market for an obscure or illiquid instrument.
  • Configuration Error: The instrument may not be mapped correctly in the bank's internal instrument database.
